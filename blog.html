Anger, Pain, and Suffering

Posted on September 25, 2015

Unless somebody leads, nobody will. Yesterday, I was riding the MAX home and towards the end of my ride, the overweight 50-year old lady next to me gets up and says, “Shame on you” as she left, her finger pointing at my face. First, I was puzzled. Then I realized it must have been because I did not give up my seat for another old, homeless-looking lady earlier. It struck something in me. I wanted to make excuses such as I have arthritis in my left ankle, and that if I don’t take care of myself, I won’t be able to take care of other people. But after some thinking, I should have given my seat up. The world is looking for leaders, and as a young person, I have to make sacrifices, even though it will be painful. So unless somebody leads, nobody will.

Ruby Programming at Epicodus

Posted on August 21, 2015

3 weeks ago, I began a 4-month programming bootcamp in Portland, OR. The goal of the class is to learn Ruby on Rails in a 40-hour/week and pair-programming format. This means that every day, from 9-5pm, we work with a different partner to solve the daily objectives. This usually involves writing several web applications a day. I wasn’t sure what to expect going into the class. I didn’t have much time to think about it as I was admitted the week before it started. But there are all sorts of interesting people in the class with very different backgrounds, all wanting to learn code. There is a former pastor there, it’s been really interesting learning about and from him. The average age of the students is probably around 24-27 years old. The youngest probably being a few 21-year olds and a couple 40-50 year olds. All in all, Epicodus has done a pretty good job selecting students that like to work well together and they have been able to foster an environment conducive to learning where everyone collaborates to help each other. Unfortunately, even though they say not to compare yourself to other students, I find myself closer to the average or slightly below it in terms of our programming levels. I would be lying if I said I wasn’t discourage that I’m not learning as fast as others. But hopefully, something will click, and I can start progressing to be one of the faster learners. For the first 3 weeks, the skills we have learned are Ruby, writing with Atom, using the CMD prompt, deploying to Sinatra and Heroku, and PostgreSQL. Well, that’s all for now. It’s Friday and I have to finish a Code Review, which is a solo test to write a hair salon application that lets a salon owner enter stylists and clients into a database.
